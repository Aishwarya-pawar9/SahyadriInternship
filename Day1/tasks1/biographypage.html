Biography of Ratan Tata

Introduction

Ratan Naval Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. Born on December 28, 1937, he is known for his visionary leadership and contribution to the growth of the Tata Group. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded internationally and became a global player in various industries such as steel, automotive, and information technology.

Early Life and Education

Ratan Tata was born in Bombay, India, into a prominent Parsi family. He studied at the Cornell University College of Architecture and later attended Harvard Business School for the Advanced Management Program. Despite being a part of one of India's most influential business families, he was raised with values of humility and hard work.

Achievements

During his tenure as chairman of Tata Sons, Ratan Tata oversaw key acquisitions including Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel. He played a pivotal role in the launch of the Tata Nano, one of the most affordable cars in the world, and made the Tata Group a global force.

Philanthropy

Ratan Tata is a noted philanthropist, with significant contributions in areas such as education, healthcare, and rural development. He continues to lead several charitable trusts that help improve the quality of life for people in India.
